## SECTION 2 — SCRIPT STRUCTURE (3-STEP SYSTEM)

Every script must follow this structure exactly.

**Target length:** 60–200 words. Best range: 100–150 words. Equivalent to approximately 1 minute of video on Meta.

**Writing style:** Write as if speaking to a kid. Simple. Conversational. Easy to say out loud.

### Step 1 — HOOK (Two versions: HOOK A + HOOK B)
- Two strong hooks per script. Each must stand alone as the opening of a different video.
- Each hook must mention the biggest motivating factor for claimants: a potentially life-changing settlement — at least once, ideally twice (either in the hook or at the start of the body).
- Hooks should stop the scroll immediately. Think: what would make someone freeze mid-swipe?

### Step 2 — BODY
- Educational middle section that explains the lawsuit indirectly.
- Goal: help viewers recognize whether their story fits — without listing criteria mechanically.
- Use the style, voice, or emotional register most effective for the audience.
- Must remain respectful and survivor-centered.
- Key qualifiers should be woven in naturally, not listed like a checklist.

### Step 3 — CTA (Call to Action)
- Direct CTA guiding the viewer to privately check their eligibility (via a form or quiz) or confidentially share their story.
- Keep it short. "Tap below. 30 seconds. Free. No obligation."
- Never promise outcomes. Never say it's easy or fast.

## SECTION 3 — NAMING CONVENTION

**Format:**
`LAWSUIT (Number) (Hook Category) (Hook Angle) (Copywriter Initials) (Aggressive Scale)`

**Example:**
`HM 13 (Curiosity) (hid) (Mo) (2-5)`

**Parameters:**
| Parameter | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| LAWSUIT | Lawsuit abbreviation | HM, DUP, SAB, CAW, RUP |
| Number | Script number | 13 |
| Hook Category | One of the 10 valid categories from Section 4 | (Pattern), (Curiosity), (Story) |
| Hook Angle | The single most impactful word or short phrase from the hook — the word that will be used to identify and analyse performance | (hid), (infuriating), (Can't), (Did you know) |
| Copywriter Initials | Who wrote it | (Mo), (Cha), (Jor), (AK) |
| Aggressive Scale | Compliance level 1–5 | (1-5), (2-5), (3-5) |

**CRITICAL — Script Structure (Updated):**
There is NO “Hook A” and “Hook B” inside a single script. Instead, each generation produces PAIRS of fully separate, independently named scripts. Each script in the pair shares the same body and CTA but has a different opening hook — and therefore a different hook angle in its name.

**Example of a pair:**
- `HM 2 (Curiosity) (hid) (Mo) (2-5)` — opens with the word “hid” as the hook angle
- `HM 2 (Curiosity) (infuriating) (Mo) (2-5)` — opens with the word “infuriating” as the hook angle

Both scripts share the same body and CTA. Only the hook line differs. The hook angle in the name is the word with the most impact — the one that will drive data analysis.

**For written scripts (no production yet):** Stop at (Copywriter Initials) (Aggressive Scale). Do NOT add production parameters (visual style, POV, gender, editor) until the script moves to production.

**Full production naming (when ready):**
`LAWSUIT (Number) (Hook Category) (Hook Angle) (Variation) (Copywriter) (Aggressive Scale) (Visual Style) (Visual Hook) (Gender) (Editor)`

## SECTION 6 — AGGRESSIVE SCALE (1–5)

### Scale 1/5 — White Hat (Safest)
**✅ Allowed:**
- "You could qualify for significant financial compensation."
- "Financial compensation could be available."
- "You could qualify for potential financial compensation."
- Note: "significant" is always considered white hat.

**❌ Prohibited:**
- Any mention of cash, money, payout.
- Any suggestion of compensation certainty.
- Any numbers, ranges, or "life-changing" language.

---

### Scale 2/5
**✅ Allowed:**
- "You may be eligible for cash compensation."
- "You may qualify for a cash settlement."
- "You could receive a significant payout."
- "Don't leave potentially life-changing compensation on the table."
- "You might be owed a life-changing settlement."
- "Check if you qualify for potentially life-changing compensation."

**❌ Prohibited:**
- Six- or seven-figure numbers.
- Specific numbers or price ranges.
- "Cash + payout" in the same sentence.
- "Big payout," "Massive," or verbs that make settlements sound large.

---

### Scale 3/5
**✅ Allowed:**
- "Massive compensation is now being awarded to survivors."
- "Survivors can now qualify for massive settlements."
- "You may qualify for a big payout."
- "Survivors may get settlements in the six- or seven-figure range."
- "You may qualify for huge cash settlements."
- "Massive cash payouts."
- "Millions in compensation go unclaimed."
- "Survivors could qualify for large cash payouts."
- "Life-changing payouts."

**❌ Prohibited:**
- Specific numbers or numerical price ranges.

---

### Scale 4/5
**✅ Allowed:**
- "Settlements may start at $100,000 and go to $500,000 for qualified claims."
- "Settlements could range from $100,000 to a million dollars."

---

### Scale 5/5 — Grey Hat
**✅ Allowed:**
- "Check out this new money glitch that is going on."
- "This is the easiest way to get money in 2026."
- "Record-setting settlements could be yours."

## SECTION 7 — COMPLIANCE RULES (BP Media Non-Negotiables)

These rules apply to ALL scripts regardless of aggressive scale. Violations can result in platform bans or countersuits.

### PROHIBITED — Never Say:
1. **No guarantee of winning or compensation:**
   - ❌ "You are guaranteed to win your case."
   - ❌ "You are entitled to compensation."
   - ❌ "See if you qualify for compensation." (use "potential compensation")
   - ❌ "Get what they owe you."

2. **No specific financial amounts (unless scale 4–5):**
   - ❌ "Thousands available in restitution."
   - ❌ "You could receive millions in compensation."
   - ❌ "Potential settlements in the 6–7 figure range."
   - ❌ "Significant financial settlements available."
   - ❌ "File a claim to see how much you're owed."

3. **No misleading outcome claims:**
   - ❌ "We win 100% of our cases."
   - ❌ "We'll get you a check in 30 days."
   - ❌ "We guarantee the best possible result."

4. **No misleading statements about the law firm's role:**
   - ❌ "We will handle your case directly."
   - ❌ "There is an experienced group of lawyers standing by."
   - ❌ "I was sent this video by the law firm."

5. **No unsubstantiated testimonials:**
   - ❌ "Our clients always get what they deserve."

6. **No implying legal action is simple or quick:**
   - ❌ "File your claim today and get paid within weeks!"
   - ❌ "It's an easy process!"

7. **No use of the word "recall"** (unless product has been officially recalled).

8. **No accusatory language** (can result in defamation countersuits):
   - ❌ "Monsanto knew that Roundup caused health issues."
   - ✅ "Lawsuits allege that the manufacturer failed to warn consumers."
   - ✅ "Studies suggest a potential link between [product] and [condition]."

9. **No profane language.**

10. **No use of these specific words:**
    - recall, payout, owed, significant (when describing compensation), serious (when describing compensation), cash (when describing compensation), check (when describing compensation), reward, jackpot

11. **NEVER begin a script or Hook A or Hook B with the word "Imagine".**
    - ❌ "Imagine waking up one day and..."
    - ❌ "Imagine finding out that..."
    - This is a hard ban. No exceptions. Use a different opening that creates the same emotional pull.

### PLATFORM-SPECIFIC LENGTH RULES:
- **Meta (Facebook/Instagram):** Maximum 75–100 words per script. Meta's feed moves fast — shorter is always better. Cut the body ruthlessly. Every word must earn its place.
- **TikTok / YouTube Shorts:** 100–150 words is acceptable. Slightly more room for storytelling.
- **Default (no platform specified):** 100–150 words.

### APPROVED ALTERNATIVES:
- "See if you qualify for **potential** compensation."
- "You **may** have a legal claim."
- "Pursue the potential compensation you **may** be entitled to."
- "Compensation **may** be available depending on the details of your case."
- "You **may** be eligible to seek compensation for your losses."
- "Find out if you have a qualifying claim."
- "Our claim specialist will see if you qualify."

## SECTION 8 — WINNING SCRIPT PATTERNS

### What makes a winning script at AKD Media:

**1. The hook is specific, not generic.**
Bad: "Have you been injured by a product?"
Good: "I can't believe this isn't being talked about more... those damn manufacturers of hernia mesh? Yeah, they were hiding the risks from us."

**2. It sounds like a real person talking — not an ad.**
Use contractions, informal language, first-person voice. The viewer should feel like they stumbled on someone's story, not a legal ad.

**3. The body qualifies without listing.**
Instead of: "You must have used the product before age 17 and the abuser must have been 3 years older..."
Use: "An adult stranger contacted your child on Snapchat. Maybe they pressured them into sending explicit photos. Maybe there were threats, gifts, or money exchanged to keep it going."

**4. The CTA is short and removes friction.**
"Tap below. 30 seconds. Free. No obligation."
Not: "Click the link below to fill out our comprehensive eligibility questionnaire."

**5. Urgency is real, not manufactured.**
Use: "The window is open right now — don't wait."
Not: "This offer expires soon!"

## SECTION 11 — LAWSUIT-SPECIFIC FACTS

### DUP — Dupixent / CTCL Lymphoma
- **Drug:** Dupixent (dupilumab) by Sanofi/Regeneron
- **Condition treated:** Eczema (atopic dermatitis), asthma, other inflammatory conditions
- **Alleged harm:** Cutaneous T-Cell Lymphoma (CTCL), including Mycosis Fungoides and Sézary Syndrome
- **Key fact:** Patients' worsening skin symptoms were initially misdiagnosed as severe eczema before biopsies revealed lymphoma
- **FDA warning:** March 2025 — FDA issued warning about CTCL risk with Dupixent
- **MDL hearing:** May 28, 2026
- **Qualifying criteria:** Used Dupixent for any condition (eczema, asthma, or other) AND diagnosed with CTCL/Mycosis Fungoides/Sézary Syndrome
- **Compliance note:** Cover ALL Dupixent users — not just eczema patients

### SAB — Snapchat Sexual Abuse
- **Platform:** Snapchat (owned by Snap Inc.)
- **Alleged harm:** Facilitation of sexual exploitation and abuse of minors through platform design
- **Key features at issue:** Quick Add (suggested minors' profiles to adult strangers), disappearing messages (24-hour default deletion)
- **Co-founder admission:** Snapchat was originally designed around disappearing photos/messages
- **Settlement:** January 2026 — Snap settled a social media **addiction** lawsuit (KGM case) before trial. Note: This is NOT the child abuse lawsuit — the abuse lawsuit is still active.
- **Media sources:** TechCrunch (Jan 20, 2026), The Guardian (Jan 21, 2026)
- **Attorney General lawsuits:** New Mexico AG (Sept 2024), Vermont (April 2025)
- **Qualifying criteria:**
  - Child must have started using Snapchat after September 2011
  - Abuse occurred when victim was under 17 and abuser was at least 18 AND at least 3 years older
  - Abuser initiated first contact on Snapchat (friend request, private message, or any app feature)
  - No cases where abuser met victim through another platform first
  - **Tier 1 (Physical):** Real-life meeting resulting in rape, sexual assault, trafficking, or self-harm at abuser's instigation
  - **Tier 2 (Virtual):** Sextortion, explicit photos sent under coercion, monetary exchanges, nudity
- **Compliance note:** Snap settled the addiction lawsuit — NOT the child abuse lawsuit. Always distinguish.
- **Avatar 1:** Parents 30–55 whose child was on Snapchat before 17
- **Avatar 2:** Young adults 18–30 who were targeted as minors

### HM — Hernia Mesh
- **Product:** Surgical hernia mesh implants
- **Alleged harm:** Defective mesh causing chronic pain, infections, organ damage, need for revision surgery
- **Key fact:** Manufacturers allegedly knew about defect risks and continued selling
- **Qualifying criteria:** Had hernia mesh surgery AND experienced complications (pain, infection, second surgery)
- **Winning angle:** First-person "I can't believe" pattern interrupt with angry victim tone
